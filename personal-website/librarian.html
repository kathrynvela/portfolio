Kathryn's Librarian Experience

My goal as a librarian is to help people find and use the information they need in an efficient and ethical way.

I am currently the Research Health Science Specialist in the Evidence Synthesis Program with the Portland VA Health Care System. I collaborate on rapid reviews of health care topics, including writing the search strategy, assisting with screening, and writing the final report.
I have a BS in biology from Washington State University Vancouver and an MLIS from San Jose State University. I am currently the Chair of the Pacific Northwest Chapter of the Medical Library Association and co-chair of the planning committee for the Mountain Pacific Health Science Libraries Conference.

Key Skills

Instruction

I have three years of experience teaching health science students and health professions various information skills through one-shot workshops, in-class sessions, and individual instruction. Topics of instruction have included database searching, citation management, and data management. While at WSU, I piloted a series of online workshops to improve the accessibility of library instruction for all students and faculty.

Outreach

I have experience with multiple forms of outreach to both health science students and health practitioners. I have managed social media accounts for an academic library on Twitter, Facebook and Instagram. I have also staffed exhibit tables at student fairs and health professions conferences. I am practiced in reaching out to key partners to share information about services and resources, and in the process build a netowrk that will support continued outreach to target populations.

Evidence Synthesis

I have one year of experience as part of an evidence synthesis team for the Veterans Health Administration. My role is to conduct systematic searches on a wide range of health-related topics. In addition, I assist with screening, quality assessment, data extraction, and report writing. As part of our COVID-19 research efforts, I lead a team to maintain a website of evidence syntheses on COVID-19 topics.

Data Management

I am versed in data management best practices and have designed instructional tools for researchers to help improve data management practices at WSU. I have completed both Research Data Management offered by the National Network of Libraries of Medicine. During my tenure at WSU, I received $17,000 in grant funds to create a research and collaboration space in the library called the Research Hub in order to support data management instruction.

Project Management

I have managed multiple projects both on my own and as part of a team. Many have already been mentioned, like the creation of the Research Hub, the development of an interprofessional book club, and a website to promote access to COVID-19 evidence synthesis. Each of these projects have given me the opportunity to practice my leadership and project management skills, including clear communication, detailed organization, and delegation to the right people.

Experience

Health Sciences Librarian

Washington State University

Spokane, Washington

May 2018 - May 2019

Summary

In May 2018, my previous jointly-funded position became fully funded by WSU Spokane and I concentrated soley on providing liaison library services to the speech and hearing sciences, nutrition and exercise physiology, and newly launched medical programs. I provided classroom instruction about evidence-based practice, using citation management software, and in-depth database searching, in addition to delivering a series of online library workshops. I regularly provided one-on-one reference consultations with students, faculty, staff, and clinicians while also offering my assistance with expert literature searches. To improve research data management (RDM) practices on campus, I launched a grant-funded collaborative learning space called the Research Hub where I offered instruction and consultations related to RDM. I also created an interprofessional book club with my colleague to foster discussions around diversity and empathy in patient care.
